    Allergy or Intolerance?

    Many people confuse food allergies with food intolerance (like lactose intolerance). Understanding the difference is very important for health management.

    Understand the Key Difference

    What happens?

    The immune system mistakenly identifies protein in milk (Casein or Whey) as a dangerous threat and attacks back.

    Critical Symptoms:

    • • Red rash & hives
    • • Swelling of lips, tongue or throat
    • • Difficulty breathing (Anaphylaxis)
    • • Vomiting immediately after drinking

    Note: Even a small amount can be fatal if not treated immediately.

    What happens?

    The body lacks the enzyme Lactase to break down milk sugar (Lactose). This undigested sugar ferments in the large intestine.

    Common Symptoms:

    • • Bloating & gas
    • • Diarrhea
    • • Stomach cramps
    • • Nausea but rarely vomiting

    Most Asian adults have some level of lactose intolerance.

    "Knowing the difference can save your life."

    🧬 Science Info

    Did You Know? (Cross-Reactivity)

    "If you are allergic to cow's milk, you have a 90% chance of being allergic to goat's milk."

    This is called 'Cross-Reactivity' where proteins in different substances are detected as the same thing by your immune system.

    Allergy-Free Zone at Home

    Transform your home into a safe environment.

    🛏️

    Bedroom

    Wash sheets with hot water 60°C every week to kill dust mites.

    🪟

    Curtains

    Use thin curtains or blinds that don't trap dust.

    🛋️

    Living Room

    Use wood or tile flooring. Avoid thick fluffy carpets.

    🧼

    Air Quality

    Use HEPA air purifiers to filter pollen and pet dander.

    Tips for Dining Out

    01
    Ask About Ingredients in Detail

    Don't be shy to ask if the sauce contains belacan, nuts or gluten.

    02
    Watch for Cross-Contamination

    Ask if the oil used for frying prawns is used to fry other food.

    03
    Bring an Allergy Card

    Prepare a written note (Malay/English) for chef reference.

    How Do Allergies Happen?

    🦠

    1. Exposure

    Allergen (dust, pollen, or food protein) enters the body. The immune system begins to "recognize" this substance as an enemy.

    🛡️

    2. IgE Antibodies

    The body produces specific antibodies called Immunoglobulin E (IgE) that attach to mast cells (immune cells).

    💥

    3. Histamine Reaction

    Upon re-exposure, IgE triggers the release of Histamine, a chemical that causes inflammation and allergy symptoms.

    Medical Technical Info

    IgE is a type of protein produced by the immune system to fight parasites. However, in individuals with allergies, IgE is produced excessively against harmless substances (like nuts or cats). It acts like a "security guard" always ready to attack.
    Histamine works to dilate blood vessels so that white blood cells can reach the "infection" site quickly. The side effect is that the skin becomes red, swollen, and itchy because skin nerves are aggressively stimulated by this chemical.
