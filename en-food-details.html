Food Allergy

Food allergy is an immune system reaction that occurs immediately after eating certain foods. Even in very small amounts, it can trigger serious signs and symptoms.

Allergy Symptom Levels

Moderate (Eyes & Skin)

  • Itchy hives
  • Red, watery or swollen eyes
  • Tingling or swelling of lips/tongue

Emergency (Anaphylaxis)

  • Shortness of breath or wheezing
  • Sudden drop in blood pressure
  • Fainting or loss of consciousness

2 Hidden Dangers

Cross-Contamination

Occurs when safe food comes into contact with allergen residue through shared utensils or frying oil.

Hidden Allergen Names

Milk may be labeled as Casein. Egg as Albumin. Always be aware of scientific terms.

Allergy Management Strategies

A

Always Be Prepared

Carry antihistamine medication or an EpiPen (if prescribed by a doctor) wherever you go.

B

Emergency Action Plan

Ensure family members or colleagues know how to act if you experience an anaphylactic attack.

C

Regular Check-ups

Undergo blood tests (IgE) or Skin Prick Tests with an immunologist once a year.
