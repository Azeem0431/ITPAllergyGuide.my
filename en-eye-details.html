🧬 Types of Eye Allergy

Seasonal Allergic Conjunctivitis (SAC)

Most common. Caused by airborne pollen that increases during certain seasons.

Perennial Allergic Conjunctivitis (PAC)

Occurs year-round. Main causes: House dust (dust mites), mold, or pet dander.

Contact Allergic Conjunctivitis

Irritation due to chemicals in makeup, contact lenses, or certain eye drops.

Main Allergy Symptoms

🔥 Extreme Itchiness
🏜️ Gritty/stinging sensation
☀️ Light sensitivity (Photophobia)
🎈 Swollen eyelids

Quick Care Guide

✅ Do

  • • Wash face with cold water after being outside.
  • • Use preservative-free artificial tears (Lubricant).
  • • Wear sunglasses to avoid dust and glare.

❌ Avoid

  • DON'T rub your eyes! It releases more histamine.
  • • Stop using contact lenses temporarily.
  • • Avoid sharing face towels with others.
🧴
👓

Contact Lens Lover?

Allergens easily stick to silicone contact lenses. If your eyes start itching, don't force it. Switch to glasses to give your cornea a break.

Safety Note

❄️ Cold Compress Tip

Keep a metal spoon in the refrigerator for 10 minutes. Apply the back of the spoon to the eye. The cold temperature helps constrict swollen blood vessels instantly!

When to See a Specialist?

  • ! Vision suddenly becomes blurred.
  • ! Sharp pain inside the eyeball.
  • ! Thick fluid/pus discharge (green/yellow).
